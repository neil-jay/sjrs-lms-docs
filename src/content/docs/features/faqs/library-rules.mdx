# Library Rules & Policies - FAQ

## What are the library hours?

The library is open:

| Day | Hours |
|-----|-------|
| Monday - Friday | 8:00 AM - 10:00 PM |
| Saturday | 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM |
| Sunday | 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM |

## Can I access the library after hours?

Library access is restricted to **operating hours**. Some areas may have extended access for specific user groups (like graduate students or faculty), but this must be **arranged in advance** with the library administration.

## What is the policy on food and drinks in the library?

Food and drinks policies vary by library section:

- **Covered drinks:** Generally allowed in most areas
- **Food:** May be restricted to designated areas

**Please respect the library environment and clean up after yourself.** Check with library staff for specific policies in your area.

## Can I use my phone in the library?

Phone use is generally allowed, but **please follow these guidelines**:

1. **Keep conversations quiet and brief**
2. **Set your phone to silent or vibrate mode**
3. **Step outside the library for longer calls** to avoid disturbing other users

## Can I study in groups in the library?

Yes, group study is allowed **in designated areas**. Please:

1. **Keep noise levels appropriate**
2. **Respect other library users**
3. Note that **some areas are designated as quiet zones** where group discussions are not permitted

## Are there quiet study areas in the library?

Yes, the library typically has:

- **Designated quiet zones** for individual study (marked clearly)
  - Users must maintain silence
- **Group study areas** (separate spaces)
  - Allow for discussion at appropriate volume levels

## What is the policy on photocopying and printing?

Photocopying and printing services are available in the library:

- There may be **charges per page**
- You must **comply with copyright laws**
- Some materials may have **restrictions on copying**

Check with library staff for **current rates and policies**.

## What are the rules for using library computers?

Library computers are available for **academic and research purposes**. Users must:

1. **Follow the library's acceptable use policy**
2. **Respect time limits** if applicable
3. **Not engage in activities** that violate institutional policies or laws

## What should I do if I find a valuable item in the library?

Turn in any found items to the **library desk immediately**. The library will:

1. Document the item
2. Hold it for a reasonable period
3. Attempt to return it to the owner if identifiable

## Is there an appeals process for decisions?

Yes, if you dispute any library decision (fines, account restrictions, etc.):

1. **Contact the library administration**
2. **Provide details** of your concern
3. The library will **review and respond** to your appeal

The appeals process may take time, but your concerns will be addressed professionally.

## What is the policy on academic integrity in the library?

The library supports academic integrity. Users must:

1. **Properly cite all sources** used from library materials
2. Avoid **plagiarism and academic dishonesty** (serious violations)
3. Use the **resources and guidance provided** by the library on proper citation and research ethics

## Are there any accessibility accommodations?

Yes, the library provides accommodations for users with disabilities. If you need assistance or have special requirements:

1. **Contact the library staff**
2. Inform them of your **specific needs**
3. The library will work to **provide appropriate accommodations**

Examples include accessible flooring, large print materials, or assistive technology.

## Who do I contact if I have concerns or complaints?

For concerns, complaints, or suggestions:

1. **Speak with library staff in person**
2. **Contact the library administration** if needed
3. Submit a **formal complaint** if the issue is not resolved

The library values user feedback and works to address concerns promptly.
