# Special Services - FAQ

## Are there special borrowing privileges for research projects?

Yes, special borrowing arrangements may be available for:

- Research projects
- Theses
- Dissertations

**Process:**

1. **Contact the library administration**
2. **Discuss your specific needs**
3. Request **extended loan periods** or special access

These arrangements are typically **granted on a case-by-case basis**.

## What is the policy on interlibrary loans?

Interlibrary loan services **may be available** to borrow materials from other libraries:

- **Additional fees:** Typically apply
- **Processing time:** Longer than regular borrowing
- **Availability:** Depends on partner institutions

**To request:**
1. Contact the library staff
2. Ask about interlibrary loan procedures
3. Inquire about availability for your needs

## Can I request a book that's not in the library?

Yes, you can submit a **book request**:

**Options:**
1. Submit through your **account dashboard**
2. Or **contact the library staff**

**The library will:**
1. Review your request
2. Consider acquiring the book if it aligns with:
   - Collection development policy
   - Budget availability
   - Institutional priorities

## What if a book I need is not in the library?

You have several options:

1. **Request the library to acquire it**
   - Follow the book request process
   - Library may purchase if budget allows

2. **Interlibrary loan**
   - Borrow from other libraries
   - May involve fees and longer wait times

3. **Special access**
   - Contact library staff to discuss alternatives
   - They may have special arrangements

## Can I donate books to the library?

Yes, the library accepts book donations:

**Process:**
1. Contact the **library administration**
2. **Discuss donation policies**

**Important to know:**
- The library **evaluates all donations** based on:
  - Relevance to collection
  - Physical condition
  - Collection development needs
- **Not all donations are accepted**

## What happens if I donate a book?

When you donate:

1. The library **evaluates the book** for inclusion
2. **Accepted books:**
   - May be added to the collection
   - Cataloged and made available to users
3. **Rejected books:**
   - May be offered for recycling or disposal
   - Or returned to donor if requested

The library thanks all donors for their support.

## How do I support the library?

There are several ways to support the library:

1. **Donate books** - Submit materials for consideration
2. **Volunteer** - Ask about volunteer opportunities
3. **Provide feedback** - Help improve services
4. **Participate in programs** - Attend library events and workshops
5. **Contact administration** - Inquire about other support options

## Are there research consultation services?

Many libraries provide research consultation services:

1. **Subject librarians** - Specialize in specific fields
2. **Research sessions** - One-on-one guidance on:
   - Database searching
   - Resource discovery
   - Citation methods
3. **Workshops** - Group training sessions

Contact the library to **inquire about availability**.

## Can I get help with my dissertation or thesis?

Yes, libraries typically offer support for:

- Literature reviews
- Database searching
- Citation management
- Accessing specialized resources required for your research

**Reach out to:**
1. Subject librarians in your field
2. Graduate student services
3. Library administration

They can **provide specialized guidance** for your project.

## Are there accessibility accommodations for research?

Yes, the library provides accommodations for:

- Users with disabilities
- Those with specific research needs
- Individuals requiring specialized formats

**Available options may include:**
- Large print materials
- Accessible computer workstations
- Assistive technology
- Extended loan periods for research materials

**Contact library staff** to discuss your specific needs.

## What if I need materials in a specific language or format?

Contact the library about:

1. **Language availability**
   - Foreign language materials
   - Translated versions

2. **Format options**
   - Large print
   - Audio books
   - E-books
   - Braille materials

The library works to **support diverse user needs** and may be able to **obtain specialized materials**.

## Can the library help with publishing my work?

Some libraries offer services such as:

- **Open access publishing support**
- **Repository submission assistance**
- **Citation impact analysis**

Ask the library about:
- Publishing support services
- Institutional repositories
- Open access resources

## How do I suggest a new service or resource?

Your suggestions are valuable! To recommend a new service or resource:

1. **Speak with library staff**
2. **Submit formal suggestions** through:
   - Library website suggestion form
   - Direct email to administration
   - In-person at the library desk

The library **values user feedback** and regularly considers suggestions for improvements and new services.
